BBQ Cauliflower and Chickpea Tray Bake

Colorful, easy to make, and bursting with flavor:
that is what these BBQ cauliflower and chickpea-filled tortillas are all about. The smokey marinade pairs especially well with the garlicky sauce and crispy red onions. We would definitely suggest adding a couple of slices of creamy avocado and a squeeze of zesty lime juice.

Ingredients

Tray bake

  • ½ head cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 cups (328 g) cooked chickpeas
  • 1 medium sweet potato, peeled, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • ½ cup (120 mL) barbecue sauce, divided
  • 1 tsp (2 g) ground cumin
  • 1 tsp (3 g) garlic powder
  • 1 tsp (2 g) cayenne pepper
  • ½ tsp smoked sweet paprika powder

Assembly

  • ½ cup (120 mL) unsweetened soy yogurt
  • 1 Tbsp (4 g) ranch spice mix
  • ¼ cup (60 mL) barbecue sauce
  • 12 small soft tortillas
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • ¼ medium red onion, thinly sliced

Directions and Tips

  1. Preheat the oven to 390°F (200°C) and line a baking tray with parchment paper.
  2. Add all the traybake ingredients to the baking tray and mix well.
  3. Bake on the center rack of the preheated oven for 40 - 50 minutes, or until the veggies are tender.
  4. Stir at least once halfway.
  5. Meanwhile, make the yogurt sauce by mixing together the yogurt and ranch seasoning in a small bowl.
  6. Once the traybake is done baking, add the remaining BBQ sauce and stir to coat.
  7. Serve inside the warmed tortillas, topped with sliced avocado, onion, and the ranch sauce.

The BBQ cauliflower and chickpea filling can be stored separately from the tortillas and sauce in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. The garlic yogurt sauce can be stored in the fridge for up to 2 days.
